get inspired, learn best practices, develop skills, validate ideas, and begin to build your team and product.

    2. LAUNCH-STAGE

    In this stage, entrepreneurs establish and formalize the company, develop the product, get feedback from customers, and prepare for the next step.

    1.Start

    a.Establish: Law firms & banks that specialize in helping startups 

    b.Workspace: Co-working and flexible workspaces (note: “official” office space goes in 3.1.a. Infrastructure)

    2.Develop 

    a.Formalize: Accounting, development and HR for early-stage startups.

    b.Prepare for Seed: Incubators and advanced mentorship. (i.e. advanced knowledge sharing, later stage events and resources for startup TEAMS) 

    3.Launch

    a.Seed Accelerators: Seed funding mentor programs (Techstars-style programs that provide funding)

    b.Pitch & Demo: Show crypto startups for investment (i.e. demo days for companies seeking seed investment) 

    3. GROWTH-STAGE

    Here, a startup proves their utility, receives recognition, and scales up. This usually requires funding, angels, VCs, and ways to connect them to startups.

    1.Recognition

    a.Investor Networking:  Connect professional investors with founders. (i.e. events or groups that facilitate connections with professional investors vs. angels) 

    b.Major Media: Mainstream crypto business press. (i.e. major crypto or regional publications that frequently champion crypto businesses) 

    2.Funding

    a.Angels / Micro-VCs: Seed-stage investors 

    b.Venture Capitalists: Series A and beyond

    3.Growth

    a.Infrastructure: Office space, HR, crypto business insurance. (i.e. office space/ HR/ insurance providers for capital-rich companies to grow and scale)

    b.Expansion: Growth accelerators/consultants. (i.e. programs and business consultants for capital-rich companies to grow and scale)
